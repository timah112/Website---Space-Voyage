Venus

Venus

The second (2nd) planet from the sun, Venus is terribly hot. The atmosphere is toxic. The pressure at the surface would crush and kill you. Scientists describe Venus’ situation as a runaway greenhouse effect. Its size and structure are similar to Earth, Venus' thick, toxic atmosphere traps heat in a runaway "greenhouse effect." Oddly, Venus spins slowly in the opposite direction of most planets.

Profile:

  • Named for: Roman goddess of love and beauty
  • Equatorial Diameter: 12,104 km
  • Polar Diameter: 12,104 km
  • Equatorial Circumference: 38,025 km
  • Orbit: 225 Earth days
  • Day: 241 Earth days
  • Surface Temperature: 462 °C
  • First Record: 17th century BC

Facts:

  • A day on Venus lasts longer than a year
  • Venus is often called the Earth’s sister planet
  • Venus is the second brightest object in the night sky
  • Atmospheric pressure on Venus is 92 times greater than the Earth’s: it's the hottest planet in our solar system
  • Venus is also known as the Morning Star and the Evening Star
  • At one point it was thought Venus might be a tropical paradise
